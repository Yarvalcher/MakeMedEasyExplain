Imagine your airways are like a finely tuned fortress. When an "unwanted guest" (allergen) floats in, the fortress's inner "lining" (airway epithelial cells) immediately goes on alert. These lining cells start an energy boost and tighten their internal connections, even sending out a specific "call for backup" (CCL26).

Meanwhile, special "guard cells" (mast cells) and "reinforcement squads" (basophils) rush to the scene. The guard cells release powerful "alarm signals" (IL-5, IL-18), while the reinforcement squads, guided by the lining's calls, send out their own "distress flares" (IL-4, IL-13).

All these signals combined create a full-blown "emergency alert" inside the fortress, leading to swelling and trouble breathing. The specific emergency response can vary, like different fortresses having unique defense strategies.
