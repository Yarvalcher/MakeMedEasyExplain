Imagine your body has two main "stress hormones" on a seesaw: **Cortisol** on one side, pushing things up when you're stressed, and **DHEA-S** on the other, helping to balance and bring things back down.

While current research doesn't explicitly show that *perceived stress at work* directly lowers your DHEA-S (the "balancer"), here's what we know about the seesaw:

1.  **Critical Balance:** A healthy seesaw is vital for your body's stress system. If your "balancer" (DHEA-S) is too low, it's like one side of the seesaw is stuck high, linked to worsened outcomes in areas like obesity, chronic pain, and aging.
2.  **Stress Tips the Seesaw:** General perceived stress often pushes the "stress-up" side (Cortisol) higher. But even then, the "balancer" (DHEA-S) doesn't always show a clear dip in response to symptoms, like in some brain studies.
3.  **Social Support:** The effect of social support (which can help with stress) on your DHEA-S "balancer" isn't always a strong, direct connection across all populations.
