Think of loneliness as a central, well-worn bridge in our minds. For many young people, this bridge is a main path, allowing feelings of deep sadness to connect directly with the pressures and challenges of their daily lives. Focusing on strengthening or closing off parts of this 'loneliness bridge' could be a key way to help.
PMID: 42384048

Over the last 10-20 years, this 'loneliness bridge' seems to be getting busier. Studies show that feelings of deep sadness have increased by over 20%, and stress has climbed by nearly 8%, like a thermometer steadily rising. Anxiety, too, remains stubbornly high, like a constant background noise. Even if there's a slight dip after big events, the presence of loneliness and general mental strain stays elevated for many, affecting even those in vulnerable situations, like older patients dealing with severe illness.
PMID: 42306205, PMID: 42215892
