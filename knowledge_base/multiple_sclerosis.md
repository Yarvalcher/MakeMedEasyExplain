Imagine your body's nervous system is like a huge network of **electrical cables** (nerve cells) that send messages all over, telling everything what to do.

These important cables are normally covered in a protective, rubber-like **insulation** (myelin) that helps the messages travel super fast and clearly.

With MS, your body's own **security guards** (immune system), who are usually busy protecting you from invaders, get confused. They mistakenly see this vital insulation as an enemy and start attacking it, stripping it away.

Without this insulation, the messages on the cables slow down, short-circuit, or even get completely lost, much like a frayed electrical wire. This leads to mixed-up signals and communication problems, causing symptoms like muscle weakness, blurry vision, or numbness, because different parts of your body aren't getting clear instructions.
