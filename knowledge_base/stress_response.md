This study didn't look at "hyper analyzing" as a cause of stress for corporate workers. Instead, it was like observing how soldiers-in-training coped during a grueling, 6-day emergency drill. Researchers found that under this intense stress, their eyes, like tiny, precision cameras, showed signs of exhaustion: the subtle, automatic movements eyes make to stay focused became less precise, similar to a shaky cameraman struggling to keep a shot steady in the morning. Meanwhile, their sleep patterns shifted, with bodies pushing for more "deep repair" and "dream-state" sleep (like a car spending more time in essential service modes) and less "light" sleep, as if trying harder to recover. This showed how fatigue builds up in military medical students during extreme training.
