Imagine your eye as a super-advanced digital camera, constantly filming the world and sending the footage to your brain, which acts like the ultimate director.

Your eye camera sits in a tough, protective **bony cup (socket)** and is moved around by small **control ropes (extraocular muscles)**.

Inside this camera:
Light enters through a clear **front window (cornea)**, passes through an adjustable **opening (pupil)**, and then is perfectly focused by a **lens**. This focused image lands on a **back screen (retina)**, which is covered with millions of tiny **light sensors (photoreceptors)**. These sensors have special **color filters (pigments)**, specifically tuned for red, green, and blue, allowing you to see all the colors of the rainbow. All the visual data from this screen is then bundled up and sent down a main **data cable (optic nerve)** directly to your brain. The entire eyeball is like a layered ball, protected by three main **coats (tunics)**.
