Imagine your body as a well-guarded fortress. The immune system is its entire security team – a vast network of guards (white blood cells), watchtowers (organs), and communication signals (proteins, chemicals). Their job is to defend against invaders like viruses and bacteria, and help repair any damage. This team has two main branches: a "Standing Army" that immediately attacks any general threat it sees, and a "Special Ops Unit" that learns specific details about new invaders, develops targeted strategies, and remembers them for next time. If the security team malfunctions, it might mistakenly attack the fortress's own residents, causing internal damage.
