Imagine your mind has a personal watchtower, always scanning for danger.

1.  **Ancestral Trauma:** If your ancestors faced terrible sieges, their watchtower was built on ground still shaky from those old battles. Even if repaired, it has hidden weaknesses.
2.  **Current Conflict:** A new war erupts, creating a fresh threat.
3.  **Heightened Sensitivity:** Because of those old, shaky foundations, your watchtower's alarm system becomes *hair-trigger sensitive*. A distant rumble might sound like the *old, devastating siege* all over again, making every new threat feel intensely familiar and terrifying.
4.  **War of Attrition:** If this new conflict drags on, it's like a relentless, exhausting siege. Your watchtower crew grows weary, the walls start crumbling, and hope drains away. This constant pressure can lead to **PTSD**, where the alarm keeps blaring even when the immediate threat lessens.
5.  **Resilience:** To keep your watchtower standing strong, you need strong community builders and government support—like bringing in fresh guards, reinforcing the walls with new stone, and ensuring everyone inside feels safe and has good leadership. That's how you rebuild its strength against future storms.
