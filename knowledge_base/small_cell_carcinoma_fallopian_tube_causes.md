Imagine your body's cells are like tiny, bustling factories.

This rare fallopian tube cancer starts when just *one* factory goes rogue, then copies itself over and over.

*   First, the main instruction manual has a typo: the `TP53` gene, which acts like the **factory's security guard** that stops problems, is now broken. The guard is asleep!
*   Next, the factory's **repair crew** (other genes like `PALB2` and `CHEK2`) is also faulty, or they've lost their specialized tools (like a broken repair kit). This means they can't fix mistakes in the manual.
*   Finally, a whole **section of important blueprints** is completely missing.

With a sleeping guard, a broken repair crew, and missing blueprints, this rogue factory can't fix its errors and just keeps growing out of control.
