Imagine your body has a highly trained castle defense system. With allergies, this system makes a big mistake:

1.  **Mistaken Identity:** It wrongly identifies a harmless visitor – like a certain food, medicine, or even something in the air – as a dangerous invader.
2.  **Overkill Response:** Instead of a simple "no entry," the defense system goes into full panic mode:
    *   **Full Castle Alert (Anaphylaxis):** Sometimes, the entire castle's alarms shriek, and all defenses launch at once, causing a body-wide emergency.
    *   **Choking Hallways (Asthma):** In your airways, it's like the walls of a hallway get agitated and drop tiny "distress flares." These flares summon hyperactive "cleanup crews" that start throwing sticky "webs" and harsh "cleaning agents" around, making the hallway narrow, sticky, and irritated.
    *   **Wall Attack (Skin Reactions):** Other times, the defense might mistakenly launch a messy paint attack or put up prickly defenses on the castle walls (your skin), causing rashes or swelling.
3.  **Specific Battle Plans:** Each type of defense response uses a particular "secret code" or "battle plan" (like IL-10 for allergies), different from the plans used for actual enemy attacks.
