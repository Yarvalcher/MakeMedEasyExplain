Imagine your body in middle age like a well-oiled machine. Regular exercise acts like a dedicated maintenance team.

If your machine has both weakened parts and a lot of extra weight dragging it down (sarcopenic obesity), this team helps shed the excess baggage (adiposity) and gets all the moving gears (functional outcomes) running smoothly again.

For your brain, which is like the machine's control panel, combining strength and cardio workouts is like installing a powerful system upgrade. This upgrade helps sharpen its overall processing speed and memory (global cognitive function), especially if the panel has started experiencing minor glitches (physiological or neurocognitive disorders).
