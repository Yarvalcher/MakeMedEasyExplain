Imagine your mind is a high-performance engine, constantly running to keep up with demanding tasks.

**Sleep** is like the essential "overnight maintenance crew" for that engine. They come in, clean out carbon buildup, check all the fluids, fix tiny wear-and-tear issues, and fully recharge the battery.

**Stress** is like running that engine at maximum power, tackling steep hills and heavy loads, day in and day out.

When your engine consistently misses its overnight maintenance (poor sleep), it's not properly prepared. So, even the usual demands (stress) feel much harder to handle, causing the engine to sputter, overheat, or even break down faster. The less maintenance you get, the more every challenge feels like a major breakdown.
